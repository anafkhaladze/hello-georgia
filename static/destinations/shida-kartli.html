About Shida kartli

Shida Kartli is a historical-geographic region in central eastern Georgia, formerly known as Zena Village. Rich in cultural heritage and stunning landscapes, it has been inhabited since the Early Bronze Age. The region boasts vast fields, valleys, waterfalls, and diverse flora, making it both historically and naturally remarkable.

History

Shida Kartli, a historic region in central Georgia, has been inhabited since the Paleolithic era. It played a key role in the formation of the "Mtkvari-Arax" culture and became an administrative center under King Parnavaz. Conquered by Rome in the 1st century BC, it remained a stronghold of Iberia, later adopting Christianity in the 4th century. Shida Kartli flourished culturally and economically in medieval times. Today, it includes Gori, Kaspi, Kareli, and Khashuri, while parts of historic Shida Kartli lie within the South Ossetian Autonomous District.

Culture

Kartli is rich in folklore, traditions, and historical sites like Surami Castle. Celebrations such as Atocoba and Didgoroba feature rituals and festivities. The region is known for its diverse cuisine, winemaking, and abundant fruit cultivation.

Geography

Shida Kartli, in eastern Georgia, has Gori as its administrative center, with other towns like Khashuri, Kareli, and Kaspi. Tskhinvali and Java remain under Russian occupation. The region features diverse landscapes, including the Mtkvari River valley, steppe plains, forested mountains, and alpine meadows. The climate is continental, with warm summers and cold winters.
