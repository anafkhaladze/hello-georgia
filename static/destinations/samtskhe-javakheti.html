About Samtskhe Javakheti

Samtskhe-Javakheti is a region in southern Georgia and covers the territories of Samtskhe-Javakheti and Tori historical-geographical provinces. Akhaltsikhe is the administrative center. The region has a distinctive geographical location which contributes its economic and cultural relations with neighboring states for centuries. Samtskhe-Javakheti is a region of great historical-cultural significance and has kept many Christian or pre-Christian monuments. It is also rich in diverse natural monuments, fossils and resorts.

History

Samtskhe-Javakheti, part of historic Meskheti, was once a key region of southwestern Georgia. Conquered by the Ottomans in the 16th century, parts returned to Georgia in 1828-29, though some remain in Turkey. Javakheti, historically part of Kartli, saw Georgian migration under Ottoman rule, later receiving Russian and Armenian settlers. Today, it remains ethnically diverse.

Culture

Samtskhe-Javakheti is known for its cultural diversity, rich traditions, and unique cuisine. The region celebrates various festivals, such as Shotaoba and Vardziaoba. Meskhetian cuisine features distinctive breads like Somini and Bazlama, as well as mulberry-based sweets. Tenili cheese, a creamy delicacy, is a regional specialty. It is also the only place in Georgia where snails are eaten.

Meskheti is a high-altitude winemaking region, with vineyards at 900-1700 meters above sea level. Traditional clothing evolved over time, influenced by Ottoman and Armenian cultures. A rare 19th-century ceremonial dress from Meskheti is preserved in the Samtskhe-Javakheti Historical Museum.
