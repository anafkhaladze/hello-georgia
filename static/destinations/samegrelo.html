About Samegrelo

Samegrelo is a historical - geographical side of western Georgia that covers the territories between the rivers Rioni, Tskhenistskali, Enguri and the Black Sea. Its central city is Zugdidi. The Samegrelo region had a great strategic importance throughout its history, because of this, it became the object of numerous invasions by armies of the Romans, Byzantines, Mongols, Turks, Persians, Russians and others. This region is rich in cultural monuments of different eras and impressive nature, blue lakes and very beautiful canyons.

History

In ancient times, Samegrelo was part of the Kingdom of Colchis, which covered present-day western Georgia. Known as Lazika or Egrisi, its political center was Archeopolis (Nokalakevi), linked to the myth of the Argonauts and the Golden Fleece. The Romans conquered the region in the 1st century BC and ruled for nearly 400 years. By the 11th century, Georgia was unified, but by the end of the century, it was divided into three independent states, including Imereti, which governed Samegrelo.

The Dadiani family ruled Samegrelo from the 12th to the 19th century. In 1803, Samegrelo became a Russian protectorate. After Georgia declared independence in 1918, Samegrelo became part of the new democratic state. Today, it is part of the Samegrelo-Zemo Svaneti region.

Culture

Samegrelo, with its rich history and customs, has always captivated visitors. Ancient Greeks linked the Colchians to Noah’s descendants, and Herodotus suggested Egyptian origins. Unusual practices like wrapping the dead in leather and tree worship are still seen in high mountain villages. One tradition, the “share of the crow,” involves throwing eggshells filled with Ghomi and cheese onto roofs to protect chickens.

The region is also known for its folk traditions, like the Megrelian “Zari” weeping song, and the famous lullaby "Megrelian Nana," which inspired Tchaikovsky's The Nutcracker. Samegrelo’s beautiful people wear intricate garments, like the long chokha, and the cuisine is spicy, with dishes like Megrelian Sulguni, Satsivi, and Elarji. The local Ojaleshi wine, a semi-sweet red, is also highly regarded.

Geography an Climate

Samegrelo is located in central western Georgia, bordered by the Rioni, Enguri, Tskhenistskali rivers, and the Black Sea, stretching across the Kolkheti lowland and part of the Caucasus Range. The region is surrounded by Svaneti to the north, Racha to the northeast, Imereti to the east, Guria to the south, Abkhazia to the northwest, and the Black Sea to the west. Zugdidi is the administrative center, with other cities including Poti, Senaki, and Khobi.

Samegrelo has diverse landscapes, from lowlands and marshy areas to hills and valleys. The Khobistskali River is the longest, at 150 km, and the highest peak is Chitagvala at 3,226 meters. The climate varies from humid subtropical in the lowlands to moderately cold and snowy in the mountains, with average temperatures ranging from 13-19°C.
