About Imereti

Imereti, in western Georgia, is a historic region with ancient culture and diverse nature. It features remnants of Colchis, temples, waterfalls, the mystical Khvamli Mountain, and numerous caves, offering a journey into a magical world.

History

Imereti, home to ancient civilizations, was part of Colchis and later Egrisi. Kutaisi, its capital, became Georgia’s cultural and political center. After Georgia's 15th-century fragmentation, Imereti became a separate kingdom. Today, it remains an important administrative region.

Culture

Imereti’s culture is rich and diverse, celebrated through festivals like Zekaroba and Bosloba. Folklore, traditional attire, and skilled craftsmanship, such as Shrosha’s pottery, highlight its heritage. Known for hospitality, Imereti’s cuisine features walnut dishes and famous khachapuri. The region is also a major center for Georgian winemaking, producing unique varieties like Tsolikauri and Tsitska.

Geography

Imereti, in western Georgia, borders several regions and features diverse landscapes from subtropics to alpine meadows. Its center is Kutaisi, with 11 municipalities. Major rivers include Rioni and Tskhenistskali. The climate is mild in the plains and colder in the highlands.
