About Bagrati Cathedral

Best time to visit : ALL SEASON

Gelati Monastery, founded by King David the Builder in 1106, is located 11 km from Kutaisi in the Imereti region. It was a major cultural and educational center, housing prominent scholars. The monastery complex includes the Church of the Assumption, St. George’s Church, St. Nicholas Church, a bell tower, an academy, and a surrounding wall. David the Builder is buried here, and it was an autonomous center until the 15th century. After being burned by the Ottomans in 1510, it was restored by King Bagrat III. Today, Gelati is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Architecture

The Church of the Assumption is a central-domed structure with beautiful frescoes. St. George’s Church, built in the 14th century, is known for its relief ornaments, while St. Nicholas Church features a two-story design. The academy, once a place of learning, is a key part of the complex, along with several historic bell towers.
