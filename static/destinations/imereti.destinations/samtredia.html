About Samtredia

Best time to visit : ALL SEASON

Samtredia, located in Imereti, was historically a swampy forest area. It transformed after the Poti-Tbilisi railway was built in 1872, improving the climate and enabling development. By 1999, it became a small town with industrial growth. Key cultural sites include the Church of the Blessed Virgin Mary in Vazisubani, Telepisi Fortress ruins, and St. George's Church in Gamochinebuli. The municipality also has a library, a house of culture, an art gallery, and the Niko Nikoladze Museum.

Geography and Climate

Samtredia municipality is located in western Georgia, in the Imereti region, on the right bank of the Rioni River. It connects the main roads of Guria and Samegrelo and includes the city of Samtredia and several villages. The municipality borders Abasha, Lanchkhuti, Vani, Khoni, Tskaltubo, and Chokhatauri. Most of it lies in the Colchis lowland, with a small part on the Sajavakho Plateau. The climate is humid subtropical, with an average annual temperature of 14°C. January averages +5°C, and July reaches +25°C.
