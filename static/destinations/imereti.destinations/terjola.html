About Terjola

Best time to visit : ALL SEASON

Tejola Municipality, in Imereti Region, has a rich history, with evidence of human settlement dating back to the Stone Age, including caves in Terjola and Godogani. In ancient times, the cities of Chikhori and Chkhari were politically significant. The Skandi Fortress, built in the 5th century, served as a strategic point on the Silk Road. In the 17th century, attacks led to the destruction of these cities, but they were rebuilt in the 18th century. Terjola became a separate district in 1930 and a municipality in 2008. The area is known for its agriculture, particularly viticulture, horticulture, and livestock, with tea and wine factories. Notable attractions include waterfalls, castles, and caves.

Geography and climate

Terjola Municipality is located in the Imereti region, in the eastern part of the Kolkheti lowland, with its administrative center in Terjola city. It borders Kutaisi to the west, Tkibuli to the north, Tchiatura to the northeast, and Baghdati to the south. The region is rich in river networks, with the Kvirila River being the main watercourse, along with Cholaburi, Dzverula, and Tskaltsitela rivers. Terjola has diverse flora and fauna, featuring Kolkheti-type vegetation, including oak, beech, and mulberry trees. The region is home to wolves, martens, badgers, and jackals. The climate is subtropical, with cold winters and dry, hot summers, and average annual temperatures ranging from 13.9°C to 14.1°C.
