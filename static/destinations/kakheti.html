About Kakheti

Kakheti, in eastern Georgia, includes Kakheti, part of Hereti, and Tusheti. Rich in history and nature, it features ancient settlements, temples, forests, waterfalls, lakes, plains, and even a desert. Its culture, landscapes, and resorts make it a top travel destination.

History

Traces of human life in Kakheti date back to the Stone Age, between 2.3 million and 10,000 years BC. The region played a key role in the Mtkvari-Araksi and Trialeti cultures. Kakheti emerged as a territorial unit under Kakho, a son of Kartlos. Initially smaller, it expanded over time, with Ujarma as its center in the 4th century and Telavi in the 11th. After Georgiaâ€™s disintegration in the 15th century, the Kingdom of Kakheti formed, with Gremi as its capital before shifting back to Telavi in the 17th century. The kingdom lasted until the 19th century, and today, Kakheti holds regional administrative status.

Culture

Kakheti is rich in culture and traditions, with notable folklore like the songs Mravalzhamieri, Tsintskaro, and Urmuli, and "Chakrulo," which was sent into space. The region celebrates many holidays, such as Alaverdoba, Iliaoba, and Gremkalakoba, with Alaverdoba being an ancient festival featuring markets and horsemen singing "Dalai" in honor of fallen soldiers. Tush folklore and cuisine are also prominent, with traditional dishes like Khinkali, Mtsvadi, and Khavitsi, alongside famous Tushuri Gudi cheese. Kakheti is also renowned for its wine, producing varieties like Rkatsiteli, Saperavi, and Kisi.

Geography

Kakheti is located in Eastern Georgia, bordered by Kartli to the west, Russia (Dagestan) to the north, and Azerbaijan to the south. The region includes the Iori and Alazani River basins and is divided into areas like Garekakheti, Kiziki, Shiga Kakheti, and Gaghmakhari. Kakheti's landscapes vary from semi-deserts and forests to plains and meadows. The Alazani Valley enjoys a moderate subtropical climate, while the Iori plateau has a dry subtropical climate. The region includes nature reserves like Vashlovani and Tusheti National Parks. Kakheti has eight municipalities, with Telavi as its administrative center.
