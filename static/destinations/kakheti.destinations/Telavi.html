ABOUT Telavi

Best time to visit : ALL SEASON

Telavi, located in Kakheti, has a history dating back to the Late Bronze Age. It developed during the Hellenistic and Antique periods, and by the XI century, it became a prominent city. In the XVII century, it became the capital of Kakheti under King Archil II. In 1758, the School of Philosophy-Theology was established, later becoming a seminary. Telavi is a cultural and economic hub with a state university, theater, and museum. It is known for viticulture, agriculture, and food industries, surrounded by the scenic Alazani Valley and Kakheti Caucasus. The city is rich in cultural monuments and offers several resorts.

Geography and Climate

Telavi Municipality, located in Eastern Georgia's Kakheti region, is bordered by Akhmeta Municipality, Dagestan, Kvareli, Gurjaani, and Sagarejo. Its administrative center is the city of Telavi, and the municipality consists of 27 administrative units. The region lies within the Alazani plain, bordered by the Gombori ridge to the west and the Kakheti Caucasus to the northeast. The highest peak, Tsivi, reaches 1991 meters. The Alazani River is the primary water source, with other rivers like Stori, Kasiskhevi, and Lopota. The area is dominated by oak-hornbeam forests.
