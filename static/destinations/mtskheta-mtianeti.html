About Mtskheta Mtianeti

Khevi is located on the northern slope of the Caucasus mountains, in Tergi valley and also contains the valleys of Truso, Baidari, Snowtskali and Dariali. Khevi is bordered by historical regions: east - Khevsureti, south - Mtiuleti, north - Ossetia, west - Shida Kartli highlands. The main river of the Khevi is Tergi. According to the territorial arrangement of modern Georgia, Khevi is part of the Kazbegi municipality and Stepantsminda is the center of it.

Khevi has been inhabited since ancient times. Kazbegi treasures are discovered here, which dates back to the VI-V centuries BC, associated with the religious cult of the ancient tribes of the Caucasus. There are many celebrations here, including Atengenoba, Spatangelozoba, Kazbegoba, Gergetoba, etc. The nature of Khevi is very impressive with its valleys, gorges, alpine lakes and the most important glorious Mount Kazbek.

There are many interesting cultural monuments in this area, such as ancient ruins, towers, icons and temples. The Gergeti Trinity church attracts peoplesâ€™ attention the most. Besides its cultural significance, it is impressive and stunning with its unique landscapes. Khevi is a real paradise for hikers, climbers and just mountain lovers.

Khevi

Khevi, located in the northern Caucasus Mountains, includes valleys like Truso, Baidari, and Dariali. It's bordered by Khevsureti, Mtiuleti, Ossetia, and Shida Kartli. Tergi River flows through Khevi, which is part of the Kazbegi municipality, with Stepantsminda as its center. Inhabited since ancient times, Khevi is rich in cultural monuments and natural beauty, including Mount Kazbek and the Gergeti Trinity Church. The region is known for its alpine lakes, valleys, and celebrations like Kazbegoba and Gergetoba, making it a haven for hikers and mountain enthusiasts.

Ertso-Tianeti

Ertso-Tianeti, part of the Tianeti district, was once under the Kingdom of Kakheti. It held strategic and economic significance, serving as a trade route between Kartli, Albania, and Armenia. In the 13th century, it was invaded by the Mongols and later became part of Kakheti after Georgia's political collapse in the 15th century. The area is mainly inhabited by mountain Georgians, especially from Pshav-Khevsureti. Modern-day Ertso-Tianeti is in the Tianeti region of Mtskheta-Mtianeti. The terrain is mostly highland with rivers like Tianeti and Iori, as well as several lakes, including Grdzelvelebi Lake. Cultural sites like Tshrakara, Bochorma Castle, and Jaleti Church are also located here.

Pshavi

Pshavi, historically known as Pkhovi, is located in the Aragvi Gorge, covering today's Dusheti district. It is bordered by the main Caucasus ridge to the northeast, Piraketa Khevsureti to the west, Shida Kartli to the south, and Ertso-Tianeti to the east. The region is divided into two parts: Back Pshavi, from the source of Aragvi to Ortskali, and Magharoskari in the lower Aragvi. Pshavi was once part of the Kakheti province, but now falls under Dusheti district in Mtskheta-Mtianeti. The area's stunning nature is reflected in Pshavi's rich folk poetry. Vazha-Pshavela, Georgia's great poet, hails from here, and the region celebrates Vajaoba in his honor. Pshavi also has many cultural monuments, including temples, icons, and shrines like the Lashari Cross and the Ghele shrine of Tamar.

Khevsureti

Khevsureti, located in eastern Georgia, is divided into Pirikita (northeastern) and Piraketi (southwestern) parts. It is bordered by Chechnya, Tusheti, Pshavi, and Ingusheti. Known for its mountains, ravines, and mineral waters, Khevsureti has a rich warrior history. Today, it is part of Dusheti municipality in Mtskheta-Mtianeti, with popular sites like Shatili and Mutso, making it a prime destination for hikers and cultural explorers.

Mtiuleti

Mtiuleti includes Aragvi river valley and the ravines next to it - Amirtkhevi, Kharkhalkhevi, Chokheltkhevi, Khadiskhevi etc. Its extreme southern border was Mtultkari, to the north by Truso Gorge, to the east by Gudamakari, to the west by Lomisi Ridge. There are many valleys in Mtiuleti. The main is Aragvi Gorge of White (Mtiuleti) and Black (Gudamakari). Mtiuleti was called Tskhavati community firstly, Khada-Tskhavati from XIII century and Mtiuleti included Khando too.

In the 50s of the XIX century, the Arab armies with general Buga reached Tskhvati but the people expelled enemy. Mtiuleti was in Aragvi Saeristavo until 1742. According to administrative-territorial divisions, Mtiuleti is part of Dusheti municipality. Mtiuleti is rich with natural beauty and cultural monuments. Mountains and waterfalls, Khada valley towers, Lomisi temple constantly attract the visitors.

Gudamakari

Gudamakari, located in the Aragvi Gorge, is bordered by Mtiuleti, Khevi, Khevsureti, Pshavi, and Kartli. Historically part of the Aragvi Saristavo, it was mentioned in 11th-century Christian records. By the 19th century, it was part of Dusheti Mazra. Today, Gudamakari is in Dusheti municipality, Mtskheta-Mtianeti region. Known for its beautiful nature, monuments, and the Pirismzeoba festival, it is also the birthplace of writer Goderdzi Chokheli.

Ksniskhevi

Ksniskhevi, in the Ksani Gorge, was historically part of the Kingdom of Kartli. It includes Akhalgori and parts of Dusheti and Mtskheta districts. The region has mountainous terrain, forests, and cultural sites like the Kabeni and Largvisi monasteries and Tsirkoli Castle.
