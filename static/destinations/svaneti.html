Svaneti

About Svaneti

Svaneti, located in northern western Georgia, is the country's highest mountainous region. Known for its rugged peaks, cliffs, and fortress-towers, it symbolizes freedom and strength. The region boasts a rich cultural heritage with unique frescoes, traditions, and breathtaking landscapes, attracting travelers and mountain sports enthusiasts.

History

The Svans were first mentioned by Greek and Roman historians like Strabon and Plinius. Originally, they inhabited a larger area, including parts of Lechkhumi, Racha, and Samegrelo. Svaneti was part of the ancient Kingdoms of Colchis and Egrisi, later becoming an important region under the Kingdom of Kartli. In the 4th century, it allied with Lazeti and helped defend its borders. Svaneti remained independent until it was annexed by Russia in 1833. The region saw an uprising in 1875-76 against Russian rule, which led to the destruction of Khalde village. Today, it is part of Samegrelo-Zemo Svaneti and Racha-Lechkhumi-Kvemo Svaneti.

Culture

Svaneti boasts a rich culture where pagan and Christian traditions blend uniquely. It has preserved ancient public holidays like Lamproba, a pre-spring festival for good weather and harvest, with wooden lamps, fire, songs, and dances. Other notable celebrations include Uplish (Kviratskhovloba) and Kvirikoba, as well as Lipanali, a day to honor the souls of the dead. Svan folklore is rich with myths, dances, and stories of gods, goddesses, and magical creatures.

The traditional Svan hat, made of felt with a cross-like thread design, is an iconic accessory. Svan cuisine, famous for dishes like Kubdari and Chvishtari, reflects the region's livestock culture, featuring diverse meat and dairy products. Rakhi (vodka) and Svan Salt are popular local drinks and spices. Svaneti also has a long history of gold mining, often referenced in legends.

Geography

Svaneti is located in the northwestern part of Georgia, nestled on the southern slopes of the Caucasus Range. It borders Racha to the east, Lechkhumi and Samegrelo to the south, Abkhazia to the west, and Karachay and Balkaria to the north. Known as the highest populated region in the Caucasus, Svaneti is home to the 10 highest peaks in the range, including Shkhara (5,201 m), Tetnuldi (4,974 m), and Ushba (4,710 m).

Svaneti is divided into Upper (Zemo) and Lower (Kvemo) Svaneti, separated by the Svaneti Range and Latpari Pass. The administrative center of Zemo Svaneti is Mestia, while Lentekhi is the center of Kvemo Svaneti. Historically, Lower Svaneti has been owned by the Dadians of Samegrelo, while Zemo Svaneti was known as free Svaneti. The climate varies from humid in lower altitudes to cold and snowy in higher regions, with temperatures ranging from -6.4 °C in January to 16.4 °C in July.
