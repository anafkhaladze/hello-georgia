About Kvemo Kartli

Kvemo Kartli, in eastern Georgia, has a rich history, home to the earliest Europeans (1.8 million years old) and the Sakdrisi Gold Mine. The region boasts cultural monuments, diverse nature, forests, lakes, and mineral springs

History

Kvemo Kartli has an ancient history, with traces of human life dating back 1.8 million years. The first Europeans, Zezva and Mzia, were discovered in Dmanisi. Kartvelian tribes, including the Gougars, inhabited the region, forming various state units over time. In the Middle Ages, it saw the rise of the Emirate of Tbilisi, Kldekari Saeristavo, and other duchies. The Mongol invasion shifted Georgia’s borders, and from the 15th century, nomadic Turkmen and other tribes settled here. Despite invasions, Kvemo Kartli remained a key center for Christianity, with numerous churches and former royal residences.

Geography

Kvemo Kartli, in eastern Georgia, borders Kakheti, Shida Kartli, Samtskhe-Javakheti, Armenia, and Azerbaijan. Rustavi is its center, with six municipalities. The region has diverse landscapes, major rivers like Mtkvari, and a varied climate, averaging 15.3°C annually.
