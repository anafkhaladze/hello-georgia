Racha

About Racha

Racha is the historical-geographic site of Georgia that is situated in the north-east side of west Georgia. It is quite rich with cultural monuments and diverse nature. Racha is well-known with the masterpiece of Georgian architecture, Nikortsminda that is characterized with rich and sophisticated ornaments. In addition to the interesting cultural sites, you can enjoy chilly forests, alpine lakes, beautiful waterfalls and stunning views in Racha.

History

Racha, with evidence of human life dating back to the Bronze Age, has a rich history. It was once part of Takveri and later became Racha Saeristavo in the 10th century. Over time, the region changed hands, with various noble families ruling, including the Charelidze and Chkhetidze families. By the 18th century, it became part of Imereti and later the Russian Empire. Today, Racha is part of the Racha-Lechkhumi and Kvemo Svaneti region.

Culture

Racha is known for its rich culture, folklore, and traditions. Public holidays like Atagenoba and Bosloba are celebrated, and famous Rachuli songs such as "Kvedvrula" and "Rashovda" are well-known. The region is also famous for its carpentry, with unique houses built by the Rachvelebi, decorated with beautiful ornaments. Rachuli cuisine, including dishes like pot beans, Rachuli ham, and Lobiani, attracts food lovers. The region is also known for producing high-quality wines, including the semi-sweet red Khvanchkara.
