About Batumi

Batumi, located in Adjara, western Georgia, is a vibrant seaside city with beautiful beaches and scenic surroundings. It has been inhabited since the Bronze Age and has a rich history, with trade links to neighboring countries. The city's blend of ancient history, dynamic architecture, sunny beaches, Batumi Botanical Garden, delicious Adjarian cuisine, and cultural attractions make it a popular destination for visitors both locally and regionally.

Geography and climate

Batumi is the administrative center of the Adjara Autonomous Republic, divided into several administrative units, including Old Batumi, Rustaveli, and Gonio-Kvariati. Located on the southeastern coast of the Black Sea, it lies primarily south of the Gulf of Batumi and the Kakhaberi plain. The city is a major international port and features a natural lake within Batumi Park. With a humid subtropical climate, Batumi enjoys warm summers and mild winters, with an average January temperature of 7.1°C and August temperature of 23.2°C. The city is known for its subtropical vegetation, cultivated parks, and tea and citrus plantations.
