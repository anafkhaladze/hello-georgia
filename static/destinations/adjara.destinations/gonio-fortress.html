About Gonio Fortress

Best time to visit : ALL SEASON

Gonio Fortress, also known as Apsaros, is a 1st-century historical site in Adjara's Gonio resort, 12 km from Batumi. Strategically located at the confluence of the Chorokhi River and the Black Sea, it once guarded key trade routes. Controlled by the Romans, Byzantines, and Ottomans, it became a museum-reserve in 2010, showcasing artifacts from the 19th to the 1980s.

Architecture

Gonio Fortress, a 228m by 195m structure with 18 towers, dates to pre-feudal times. Layers from Byzantine and Genoese eras show its long history. Inside, stone stairs lead to towers, and underground tunnels remain. Once adorned with an ornate entrance, some artifacts were later removed by foreign archaeologists.

History

Gonio-Apsaros, linked to the Argonauts' myth, was a key Roman and Byzantine stronghold. Excavations reveal Roman baths, mosaics, and a VI-century church. It later fell to the Ottomans before joining the Russian Empire in 1878.
